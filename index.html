Research / Analytics

Every project I take always starts with some degree of research all depending on client requirements. Research is the heart and center of any UX design project. Over the years I have taken on many different projects ranging from simple interface refresh designs to concept through completion of a product.

For basic and fast turnaround projects I start with a competitive and SWOT analysis to ensure the business goals, as well as customer needs, are met.

Projects requiring development of a new product or a complete redesign of a digital platform typically involve a more sophisticated approach. This usually includes methods such as user research, personal interviews, content audits, and surveys just as a starting point.

Few Examples of My Work

Design

UX Design is all about finding the perfect place where business goals align with solutions to customer's needs. My designs are driven by user research and usability testing. I am biased towards minimalistic design and feel that every screen or object somehow tells a consistent story. This store is to be the dialog between the business and the customer through the product I design. This means the design of information architecture, visual hierarchy, heuristics, typography and every other element of the experience must communicate a consistent message that is effortlessly understood by the user.

My process starts with the design of user personas based on target users and sometimes real people to align project development and team collaboration. Next, through the process of card sorting, user flows, and storyboards I develop the information architecture and site map.

Once a basic app or website structure is in place I create simple wireframes to begin paper prototyping and testing of the designed material.

After initial usability testing, results are analyzed and the new information is used to further iterate the design into higher fidelity interactive prototypes for further testing.

Depending on whether the project is agile or waterfall the testing and iteration process can continue through product development extending beyond wireframes and mock prototypes.

Few Examples of My Work

Usability Testing

Based on the requirements of a project testing methodology can vary. Where relevant, my typical go-to method for usability testing is the traditional method of in-person observations and surveys. I really enjoy watching users interact with my work and get in-person feedback from their experience. For projects focused on the design evolution of an existing product, more common methods are A/B testing, click testing and Google analytics.

When working on both small and large project I get the best results through iteration. With every step in the design process whether it's just one screen of an app or the user flow of a website, my goal in the design process is to get my work in front of the user as early as and often as possible.
